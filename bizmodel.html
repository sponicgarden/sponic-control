Business Model

Unit economics & 12-month projection — Warsaw prototype

💶 €40 per visit 👥 250 max capacity 📈 5%/week growth 🧑‍💼 4 core staff 🏗️ €200k buildout
Assumptions

Model Inputs

Conservative base case for the Warsaw prototype. 64 founding members get their first experience free. Paying visits start at 25/day and grow 5% per week, reaching full 250 capacity by Month 12. Buildout capital is deployed starting month 6 as Phase 2 expansion begins.

💰 Revenue

  • €40 per visit (1–4 hours)
  • 250 daily capacity (max)
  • 5% weekly growth from 25 visits/day
  • 30 days/month operating
  • Steady-state monthly (M12): €300,000

📊 Ramp Schedule (5%/week)

  • Month 1: ~28 paying visits/day (+ 64 free founding)
  • Month 3: ~42 visits/day
  • Month 5: ~65 visits/day
  • Month 7: ~99 visits/day
  • Month 9: ~151 visits/day
  • Month 12: 250 visits/day (100% capacity)

🧑‍💼 Team (4 FTE)

  • Developer / Digital Product Manager
  • Event Manager
  • Physical Construction Builder
  • Operations Manager
  • Avg salary: €45k/yr = €3,750/mo each
  • Total: €15,000/month

🏢 Fixed Costs

  • Rent: €10,000/month
  • Tech equipment: €100,000 (amortized 12mo = €8,333/mo)
  • Buildout: €200,000 (months 6–12 = €28,571/mo)

Sensitivity

What-If Scenarios

🔮 Growth Rate Sensitivity

  • 3%/week: Reaches 250 at ~18 months. Year 1 revenue ~€988k, net ~€388k
  • 5%/week (base case): Reaches 250 at Month 12. Year 1 revenue €1.61M, net €1.01M
  • 7%/week: Reaches 250 at Month 9. Year 1 revenue ~€2.2M, net ~€1.6M
  • Breakeven: 28 visits/day (M1–5) or 52 visits/day (M6+ with buildout)
Price sensitivity at full capacity (250/day)

At 250 visits/day (Month 12 onward):

€30/visit: €225k revenue → €163k/mo net (72% margin)

€40/visit: €300k revenue → €238k/mo net (79% margin)

€50/visit: €375k revenue → €313k/mo net (83% margin)

€60/visit: €450k revenue → €388k/mo net (86% margin)

At full capacity, even €30/visit produces outstanding margins due to the high fixed-cost leverage.

Founding cohort economics

64 founding members receive their first visit free. At €40/visit, this represents €2,560 in foregone revenue — a negligible customer acquisition cost of €40/member for the most valuable early adopters who will provide critical feedback and become evangelists.

If each founding member returns 2x/month at €40, that cohort alone generates €5,120/month ongoing.

Capital

Startup Capital Requirements

💼 Startup Capital Needed

  • Tech equipment upfront: €100,000
  • Working capital (3-month buffer): ~€50,000
  • Total seed: ~€150,000

The model is cash-positive from Month 1 (barely — €267 net). By Month 5, cumulative profit reaches €99k. Phase 2 buildout (€200k from Month 6) requires either pre-funding or a small bridge, as cumulative profits only cover ~50% by the time it starts.

Slower ramp, higher ceiling. Unlike a flat-occupancy model, the exponential growth means Month 12 alone generates €238k net. By end of Year 1, cumulative profit exceeds €1M despite the conservative start.

Risks

Key Risks

⚠️ Demand Risk

Sustaining 5% weekly growth for 47 weeks is ambitious. Mitigated by Warsaw's low cost base — breakeven at just 28 visits/day (M1–5) means the model survives even if growth stalls early.

⚠️ Ramp Risk

5% weekly growth compounds aggressively — ~80 visits/day by Month 6, 250 by Month 12. The founding cohort's free first visit and community voting model are designed to seed organic word-of-mouth.

⚠️ Buildout Overrun

Phase 2 construction costs could exceed €200k. Cumulative profit at Month 5 is ~€99k — a shortfall vs the €200k buildout. May need a small bridge loan (~€100k) or delay Phase 2 by 2 months to self-fund.

⚠️ Variable Costs

Utilities, consumables, insurance, and vendor commissions are not modeled. These are typically 10–15% of revenue in similar venues, reducing margin to ~50–55% — still very healthy.

Growth model: 25 paying visits/day compounding at 5% per week (doubling every ~14 weeks), capped at 250 daily capacity. 64 founding members receive their first experience free — a ~€2,560 customer acquisition investment. All projections assume admission revenue only — workshop fees, F&B commissions, and premium experiences represent upside not included in this model.
