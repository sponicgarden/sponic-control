Business Model

Unit economics & 12-month projection — Warsaw prototype

💶 €40 per visit 👥 250 max capacity 📈 60% target occupancy 🧑‍💼 4 core staff 🏗️ €200k buildout
Assumptions

Model Inputs

Conservative base case for the Warsaw prototype. Revenue ramps linearly from founding cohort to 60% occupancy over 6 months. Buildout capital is deployed starting month 6 as Phase 2 expansion begins.

💰 Revenue

  • €40 per visit (1–4 hours)
  • 250 daily capacity
  • 60% occupancy at steady state = 150 visits/day
  • 30 days/month operating
  • Steady-state monthly: €180,000

📊 Ramp Schedule

  • Month 1: 64 founding members (free)
  • Month 2: ~50 paying visits/day
  • Month 3: ~75 visits/day
  • Month 4: ~100 visits/day
  • Month 5: ~125 visits/day
  • Month 6+: 150 visits/day (60%)

🧑‍💼 Team (4 FTE)

  • Developer / Digital Product Manager
  • Event Manager
  • Physical Construction Builder
  • Operations Manager
  • Avg salary: €45k/yr = €3,750/mo each
  • Total: €15,000/month

🏢 Fixed Costs

  • Rent: €10,000/month
  • Tech equipment: €100,000 (amortized 12mo = €8,333/mo)
  • Buildout: €200,000 (months 6–12 = €28,571/mo)

Sensitivity

What-If Scenarios

🔮 Occupancy Sensitivity

  • 40% occupancy (100 visits/day): €120k revenue → €58k/mo net at steady state
  • 60% occupancy (150 visits/day): €180k revenue → €118k/mo net at steady state
  • 80% occupancy (200 visits/day): €240k revenue → €178k/mo net at steady state
  • Breakeven point: ~28 paying visits/day covers all costs (including buildout)
Price sensitivity analysis

At 60% occupancy (150 visits/day), months 6–12:

€30/visit: €135k revenue → €73k/mo net (56% margin)

€40/visit: €180k revenue → €118k/mo net (66% margin)

€50/visit: €225k revenue → €163k/mo net (72% margin)

€60/visit: €270k revenue → €208k/mo net (77% margin)

The model is robust. Even at €30/visit the business is highly profitable at 60% occupancy.

Founding cohort economics

64 founding members receive their first visit free. At €40/visit, this represents €2,560 in foregone revenue — a negligible customer acquisition cost of €40/member for the most valuable early adopters who will provide critical feedback and become evangelists.

If each founding member returns 2x/month at €40, that cohort alone generates €5,120/month ongoing.

Capital

Startup Capital Requirements

💼 Pre-Revenue Capital Needed

  • Tech equipment upfront: €100,000
  • Month 1 operating (no revenue): €33,333
  • Working capital buffer: ~€16,667
  • Total seed: ~€150,000

The business generates positive monthly cashflow from Month 2. Phase 2 buildout (€200k) can be funded entirely from operating cash flow — cumulative profit hits €253k by Month 5, before buildout begins.

Self-funding expansion. The €200k buildout is comfortably covered by Month 5 profits. No external capital needed beyond the initial €150k seed for Phase 2.

Risks

Key Risks

⚠️ Demand Risk

60% occupancy may be ambitious in the first year for a new concept. Mitigated by Warsaw's low cost base — breakeven at just 28 visits/day means the model survives even at 11% occupancy.

⚠️ Ramp Risk

Reaching 150 visits/day by Month 6 requires strong word-of-mouth from founding cohort. The free-first-visit strategy and community voting model are designed to create evangelists.

⚠️ Buildout Overrun

Phase 2 construction costs could exceed €200k. Buffer: by Month 5, cumulative profit is €253k — enough to absorb a 25% overrun without external capital.

⚠️ Variable Costs

Utilities, consumables, insurance, and vendor commissions are not modeled. These are typically 10–15% of revenue in similar venues, reducing margin to ~50–55% — still very healthy.

* Month 1: 64 founding members receive free admission. Revenue begins Month 2 as founding cohort returns as paying members and new visitors join. All projections assume admission revenue only — workshop fees, F&B commissions, and premium experiences represent upside not included in this model.
