Business Model

Unit economics & 12-month projection — Warsaw prototype

💶 €24 avg per visit 👥 250 max capacity 📈 3%/week growth 🧑‍💼 4 core staff 🏗️ €200k buildout
Assumptions

Model Inputs

Stress-test scenario for the Warsaw prototype. Assumes heavy promotional discounting (40% off list price) and slower organic growth (3%/week vs 5%). 64 founding members get their first experience free. This is the "worst realistic case" — what happens if demand is soft and we have to buy our way in.

💰 Revenue

  • €24 avg per visit (40% promo discount from €40 list)
  • 250 daily capacity (max)
  • 3% weekly growth from 15 visits/day
  • 30 days/month operating
  • Month 12 revenue: €46,800 (65 visits/day)

📊 Ramp Schedule (3%/week)

  • Month 1: ~16 paying visits/day (+ 64 free founding)
  • Month 3: ~21 visits/day
  • Month 6: ~30 visits/day
  • Month 9: ~45 visits/day
  • Month 12: ~65 visits/day
  • Month 22: 250 visits/day (full capacity)

🧑‍💼 Team (4 FTE)

  • Developer / Digital Product Manager
  • Event Manager
  • Physical Construction Builder
  • Operations Manager
  • Avg salary: €45k/yr = €3,750/mo each
  • Total: €15,000/month

🏢 Fixed Costs

  • Rent: €10,000/month
  • Tech equipment: €100,000 (amortized 12mo = €8,333/mo)
  • Buildout: €200,000 (months 6–12 = €28,571/mo)

Unit Economics

Per-Visit Breakdown

At €24/visit with only 65 visitors/day (Month 12), fixed costs dominate. The unit economics only work at scale.

📥 Revenue per Visit

  • Admission (discounted): €24.00

📤 Cost per Visit (at 65/day)

  • Staff: €7.69
  • Rent: €5.13
  • Tech amort.: €4.27
  • Buildout amort.: €14.65
  • Total: €31.74

🚨 Negative Margin at M12

-€7.74 per visit — losing money on every visitor

At 65 visits/day and €24/visit, cost per visit (€31.74) exceeds revenue. The business needs 86 visits/day at €24 to break even on monthly costs (including buildout), or 46 visits/day without buildout.

🎯 Future Upside: At Full Price & Scale

If discounts are phased out and growth continues to 250/day:

  • At 250 visits/day × €40: cost/visit drops to €8.25 → €31.75 margin (79%)
  • At 250 visits/day × €24: cost/visit = €8.25 → €15.75 margin (66%)
  • Full capacity at full price reached ~Month 22
Sensitivity

Path to Profitability

🔮 What It Takes to Break Even

  • At €24/visit: Need 86 visits/day (M6+) or 46 visits/day (M1–5). Reached around Month 17–18
  • At €32/visit (20% discount): Need 65 visits/day (M6+). Reached around Month 12
  • At €40/visit (full price): Need 52 visits/day (M6+). Reached around Month 9
  • Key lever: Raising average price from €24 to €32 cuts time-to-breakeven by 6 months
Discount phase-out strategy

If the 40% promo discount is gradually phased out (€24 → €32 by M6, €40 by M12), the blended Year 1 revenue roughly doubles, turning the €293k loss into a small profit. The promotional period buys market awareness; the question is whether demand sustains as prices rise.

Cost reduction levers

Delay buildout: Removing the €200k Phase 2 buildout saves €28.6k/mo in M6–12. Year 1 loss drops from €293k to €93k.

Reduce team to 3 FTE: Saves €3,750/mo = €45k/yr. Year 1 loss drops to €248k.

Both: Year 1 loss drops to just €48k — survivable with modest seed capital.

Founding cohort economics

64 founding members receive their first visit free. At €24/visit, this represents €1,536 in foregone revenue. If each founding member returns 2x/month at €24, that cohort generates €3,072/month ongoing — meaningful when total monthly revenue is €11–47k.

Capital

Funding Requirements

💼 Capital Needed (Stress Case)

  • Tech equipment upfront: €100,000
  • Year 1 operating losses: €293,000
  • Working capital buffer: ~€57,000
  • Total funding needed: ~€450,000

This stress scenario requires ~€450k to survive Year 1. The business doesn't generate positive monthly cash flow until visits reach ~46/day (without buildout) or ~86/day (with buildout) — around Months 14–18 at 3% weekly growth.

This is a fundraising scenario, not a bootstrapping one. Unlike the base case (5%/week at €40), the stress test shows that heavy discounting + slow growth requires significant outside capital and patience. The business becomes a strong cash generator only after ~18 months.

Risks

Key Risks (Stress Scenario)

⚠️ Cash Runway

With €293k in Year 1 losses, the business burns ~€24k/month on average. Without sufficient runway, it dies before reaching the growth inflection point.

⚠️ Discount Trap

Promotional pricing at €24 may anchor customer expectations. Raising prices later risks churn. Must plan the transition from promotional to full price carefully.

⚠️ Buildout Timing

Starting €200k buildout at Month 6 — when the business is losing €40k/mo — is aggressive. Consider delaying to Month 12+ when demand is proven and cash losses are narrowing.

⚠️ Growth Stall

3%/week growth stalling below 86 visits/day means the business never reaches monthly breakeven with buildout costs. The "valley of death" between months 6–18 is the critical risk zone.

Stress-test model: 15 paying visits/day compounding at 3% per week (doubling every ~23 weeks), capped at 250 daily capacity. Average admission €24 (40% promotional discount from €40 list price). 64 founding members receive their first experience free. All projections assume discounted admission revenue only — full-price transitions, workshop fees, F&B commissions, and premium experiences represent upside not modeled.
